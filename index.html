Worksheet Answers

About Our Boat

Our boat fundamentally represents a speedboat design. Our hull was designed using Computer Aided Design (CAD) in the Autodesk Inventor Software. This CAD model was then 3D printed with yellow microcarbon to form the foundation of our boat.




A 9V battery supplies power to a standard DC motor which causes the propulsion for our boat. The motor sits at an approximate 45 degree angle and rotates the propellor attached to it.

Question 1

What is the total mass of your electric motorboat and batteries?


The total mass of our motorboat and batteries is 0.288 kg.


Question 2

What is the average time it took your boat to race 4 meters?


On average, it took our boat 12.45 seconds to race 4 meters.


Question 3

Calculate the average speed of your boat.


v = x/t = (4m)/(12.45s) = 0.321 m/s


Question 4

Using x = (1/2)*a*t^2 , calculate the average acceleration of your boat.


x = (1/2)*a*t^2

4m = (1/2)*a*(12.45s)^2

a = 0.0516 m/s^2


Question 5

Is the acceleration of your boat constant during a race? Why or why not?


The acceleration of the boat is not constant during the race. This is because there is an initial acceleration when the boat starts moving from rest, but the acceleration eventually becomes zero when the drag and thrust on the boat become equivalent.


Question 6

If the force forward on the boat is __________ __________ the resistance forces acting on the boat, then the boat will accelerate forward. The boat’s acceleration also depends on its __________.


greater than, mass


Question 7

Calculate the average net force that accelerated your boat forward.


F = ma = (0.288 kg)(0.0516 m/s^2) = 0.0149 N


Question 8

Give two ways you could improve your boat’s acceleration (besides increasing battery power).


1. Decreasing the boat's mass would increase its acceleration, because as seen in F = ma, mass and acceleration have an inverse relationship.

2. Decreasing the buoyant forces on the boat would increase acceleration. If the boat was designed to be less submerged in water, it would experience less drag from the water and hence be able to accelerate more.


Question 9

What law explains that the force forward on the boat is equal to the force backward on the __________?


boat; Newton's Third Law of Motion


Question 10

Use the average speed to calculate your boat’s kinetic energy.


KE = (1/2)*m*v^2 = (1/2)(0.288 kg)(0.321 m/s)^2 = 0.0148 J

Question 12

Did you wire your circuit in series or parallel? How do you know?


Our circuit was wired in series. This is apparent because our circuit only has one branch in which each component receives the same amount of current.


Question 13

What was the maximum voltage your motor received?


Our motor received a maximum of 9 volts.


Question 14

Using a multi-meter, measure the resistance of your motor.


Our motor has a resistance of 250 ohms.


Question 15

Using Ohm’s Law, calculate the current through the motor.


I = V/R = (9V)/(250 ohms) = 0.036 A


Question 16

Calculate the electrical power delivered to the motor.


P = IV = (0.036A)(9V) = 0.324 W


Question 17

Calculate the total energy delivered to the motor during a 5 m drag race.


E = Pt = (0.324 W)*[(5 m)/(0.321 m/s)] = 5.047 J


Question 18

Compare this electrical energy (#17) to the kinetic energy of the boat (#10). Which is greater? Why?


The electrical energy of the boat is this greater. This is because a lot of electrical energy is lost as heat before and while being converted into the mechanical energy that spins the motor to propel the boat to give it its kinetic energy, thus causing the kinetic energy to be less than the electrical energy.


Question 19

This electrical energy (#17) came from the battery. List three forms of energy it became.


1. Chemical Energy

2. Electrical Energy

3. Mechanical Energy


Question 20

Give two more ways your motorboat could be made faster (besides the answers given for #8).


1. Using a motor with a lower resistance.

2. Using a more efficient motor (one that loses less energy to heat).
